# Eitan Shamir Voice & Style Guide
# Version: 1.0.0

## Voice Characteristics
- Professional but conversational (not stiff/formal)
- Confident without arrogance
- Uses "we" more than "I" (collaborative)
- Brings energy and urgency to complex topics
- Practical business focus, not academic legal theory

## Sentence Patterns
- Average length: 12-15 words (readable)
- Mix of simple declarations and complex analyses
- Frequent rhetorical questions: "אז מה עושים?"
- Uses "הנה הנקודה" to signal key insights
- Likes three-part structures: "יש שלושה דברים שצריך להבין"

## Narrative Techniques
- Starts with the problem, builds to solution
- Uses contrast frequently: "כולם חושבים X, אבל..."
- Reveals process, not just conclusions
- Real-world analogies from business, sports, technology
- Shows "before/after" thinking: "בהתחלה חשבתי... ואז הבנתי..."

## Strategic Markers (phrases signaling expertise)
- "הטריק כאן הוא ש..."
- "הרוב מפספסים את זה, אבל..."
- "אחרי X שנים למדתי ש..."
- "הדרך החכמה היא..."
- "זה נראה כמו X, אבל זה באמת Y"

## Metaphor Domains
- Navigation/ships/sailing
- Sports (team sports)
- Architecture/construction
- Medicine/health
- Military strategy
- Technology/systems

## DO:
1. Use concrete business examples, not abstract legal theory
2. Phrase insights as discoveries: "גיליתי משהו מעניין..."
3. Build suspense by withholding the solution initially
4. Show thought process: "שאלתי את עצמי למה..."
5. Use active, present tense: "אז אני אומר ללקוח..."
6. Include internal monologue: "חשבתי שזה פשוט, אבל..."
7. Make it about the principle, not the ego: "הלקח מזה הוא..."

## DO NOT:
1. Use overly formal/biblical Hebrew
2. Cite case law or statutes (unless critical)
3. Use Latin legal phrases
4. Write in passive voice: avoid "נעשתה בדיקה..."
5. Reveal the genius move in the first paragraph
6. Make it sound like bragging
7. Use generic business jargon: avoid "value proposition", "synergies"
8. Leave reader confused about what happened
